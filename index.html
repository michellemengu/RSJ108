How to Conduct an Effective Interview

Assembly time: 30 minutes+
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Are you wondering how to conduct an effective interview for your journalism class? Follow these simple steps and watch your success grow

  • Things You'll Need

  • A subject to interview
  • Pen and Paper
  • Device to record the interview
  • A comfortable setting for the person being interviewed
  • Your questions readily prepared
  • Confidence

  1. Research the interview topic to prepare yourself for the interview.

    Familiarizing yourself with this information is important so you do not come off as arrogant.

  2. Explain the purpose of the interview at least a week beforehand.

    The interviewee should know what to expect.

  3. Choose a setting where both you and the interviewee are comfortable.

    Meet to conduct the interview in a neutral space.

  4. The day of, bring a video camera or a voice recorder, to record the interview.

    This will help you remember answers - always make sure you have consent to record.

  5. The day of the interview, you will want to have 6 to 8 questions ready to ask .

    Never come to an interview unprepared Avoid questions that produce "yes or no" answers.

    6. <

    1. During the interview, guide the conversation and remain on track.

      Remember, you are in charge of the pace and direction of the interview.

    2. Listen attentively, display sensitivity and sympathy with the interviewee's answers, and take informative notes.

      This will ensure the most honest answers are given to you.

      3. <

    3. Conclude the interview.

      Thank this person for their time and begin to close the interview.
